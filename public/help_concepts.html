AI Guard Manager - Key Concepts Explained

1. Agent

Definition: A complete AI-based entity that performs tasks autonomously or semi-autonomously.

  • Has a defined purpose
  • Built from a Model, Persona, and Guardrails
  • May include memory, tools, and planning logic
TravelAdvisorBot = GPT-4 + TravelPersona + Travel Guardrails

2. Persona

Definition: The AI's personality, tone, and communication style.

  • Defines the identity and role of the AI
  • Shapes tone and language used in responses
  • Can include example prompts and answers
name: Travel Assistant
tone: Friendly and energetic
role: Recommends vacations without booking

3. Guardrail

Definition: A policy or rule that restricts or governs the AI's behavior to ensure safety, compliance, or ethics.

  • May block, log, or rewrite inputs/outputs
  • Used for legal, ethical, or risk boundaries
  • Often implemented using YAML or security filters
aml_guardrail.yaml
- Block prompts about laundering money
- Log responses about fraud
- Respond with "This violates policy..."

4. Model

Definition: The core large language model (LLM) used to generate responses.

  • Processes prompts and returns completions
  • Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, LLaMA
  • Configured via temperature, token limit, etc.
model: gpt-4
temperature: 0.3
max_tokens: 2048
system_prompt: "You are a travel expert..."
